  1. 1. Problem definition

    Timely identification of issues that should alert and frame decision making, issue framing, management of uncertainty and complexity, exploratory identification of strategic information (not necessary leading to agenda setting): Connection to research and business goals and new paradigms.
  2. 2. Agenda setting

    Identifying issues for policy attention, structuring, priority setting, initiation of innovation processes, Build on issues in stage 1 and support policy discussion in a structured ways (however final policy decision are normally results of many divergent also non-professional factors). Stakeholder participation and communication of knowledge are important in this phase;
  3. 3. Policy development

    Policy issues are transformed into policy programs, strategies, measures etc. Here forward-looking information helps improving strategic competence & policy coherence;
  4. 4. Policy implementation

    Good policy programme doesnâ€™t ensure good outcome. Execution and enforcement (Specification of policies in term of means, responsibilities and financial resources) are crucial to deliver outcomes. Institutions, processes and other mechanisms are important in this phase. Forward-looking information can help improving strategic coordination & communication.
  5. 5. Policy evaluation

    checking of existing policies- lessons learned, new developments, and evaluation of all policy cycling phases. It provides link to research needs, to public. Stakeholder involvements is again very important, as evaluation needs to reach out of politics realm. Forward-looking information can facilitate policy adaptation and learning and establishing close link to phase 1.
